feature: **Chart widget**: `ui.chart` is a first-class data-visualization leaf — line (with optional area fill), bar, and band series drawn through the vector path pipeline with design-token colors, so charts retheme with the palette, repaint exactly when their data changes, and render byte-identically on the reference renderer (light/dark goldens pin them). Series are `[]const f32` at uniform x steps with `NaN` as a missing-sample gap; the y domain derives from the data (bars always anchor at zero) unless pinned, and opt-in token-hairline gridlines/baseline cover the minimal-axes case — axis labels stay composition.
- Budget-honest by construction: `ui.chart` downsamples every series past 256 points with deterministic index-bucket min/max decimation (spikes survive, same series always renders the same pixels), so a 10k-point star-history series draws within the 2048 path-element budget instead of erroring; retained series live in per-view storage under new loud `max_canvas_widget_chart_series/points_per_view` budgets (64 series / 16384 points).
- Automation-visible: role `chart`, a generated source-series summary as the label ("chart: stars 10000 pts last 9999.00"), and the first series' latest value as the accessibility value, so agents assert on live chart data without pixel access. Display-only — never a hit target, presses fall through like text.
- Zig-only in v1 (markup's scalar bindings cannot carry series arrays — the documented select-options constraint); the system-monitor example's three sparklines moved off their hand-built per-sample bar widgets onto single `ui.chart` leaves (~180 fewer nodes per frame), with CPU/memory as zero-baseline bars on the absolute 0..1 domain and the process count as a filled auto-domain line.
