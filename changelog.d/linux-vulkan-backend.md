feature: **Linux gpu_surface presents through Vulkan**: a gpu_surface view now scans out through an SDK-owned Vulkan renderer instead of GSK — the Metal analog on Linux. The swapchain rides a native child surface the compositor composites directly: a `wl_subsurface` on Wayland (`GpuSurfaceBackend.wayland`) or a child `InputOutput` window on X11 (`GpuSurfaceBackend.x11`), both reporting their active backend in frame events. The reference renderer's straight-alpha RGBA8 is uploaded into a canvas image and presented via a full-screen quad (shaders compiled inline at init via shaderc, mirroring the Metal host's inline MSL); input passes through the child surface to the GTK widget beneath. When no Vulkan surface backend applies (headless, unknown display, no device) the host keeps its Cairo pixel path and reports `.software`.
