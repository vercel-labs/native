fix: **Cross-centering splits overflow evenly instead of top-pinning**: a cross-centered child taller than a horizontal container's padded band used to be clamped to the band and its inner flow spilled past the bottom edge only — a two-line text stack in a fixed-height list row rendered with a visible gap above the title and the subtitle pressed against the row's bottom edge. The child now keeps its intrinsic height and the negative free extent halves, so the overflow lands evenly on both edges and the content sits optically centered; width (the cross axis of vertical containers) keeps the clamp because the offered width is what drives text wrap.
